Practice Philosophy: Psychotherapy is a collaborative effort between the client/clients and therapist in helping the client meet their personal challenges and manage themselves. I approach this from a strengths and resilence perspective focusing on people's assets rather than their deficits. The problems that I work with frequently include:marital and family conflict, life transitions, depression and anxiety, and illness and loss. My knowledge and sense of understanding come from many years of clinical practice, along with empathy and concern for my clients.

90-Minutes-to-Improve-Your-Life Workshops - Offered periodically

Art of Effective Communication: Learn the keys to effective listening, negotiating conflicts, and speaking your mind so that “the other” guy really understands you.

Understanding and Managing Your Stress: Take yourself out of the bullseye and design your personal plan to keep your cool.

Increasing Emotional Intelligence: Accurately reading and understanding emotions helps get you what you want from yourself and others.

Take Charge of Your Life: Setting and Completing Achievable Goals: Pleasantly surprise yourself with acquiring the few skills that will keep you on track to achieving your goals.
